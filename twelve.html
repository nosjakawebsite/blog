The Virgin Suicides by Jeffrey Eugenides

★★★★☆

The Virgin Suicides by Jeffrey Eugenides, I think, is very well written and does a very good job at explaining the tragic lives of the Lisbon sisters as seen in the first person of the neighborhood boys. At first, I thought the book was written by a woman, given its heavy focus on the struggles that I think many women go through. However, after discovering that the book was written by a man, it made more sense, as the story is about boys trying to understand what happened to the Lisbon sisters. I also think it adds to the idea of the male gaze. I think the word choice in the book was also very well done, with some parts being somewhat poetic in a way, which I think creates a somewhat eerie and dream-like atmosphere. However, there were some sections that I thought were complex with symbolism that could be difficult to understand on your first read. Because of this, I think that it would be best to read this book two or more times to fully understand the story.

Quote from The Virgin Suicides

"In the end, it wasn't death that surprised her but the stubbornness of life."
- Jeffrey Eugenides, *The Virgin Suicides*
